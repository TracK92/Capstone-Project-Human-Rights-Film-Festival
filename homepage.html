A film festival to celebrate humanity in all its glorious colors. We are one clan and on this day we come to stand for each other because human rights matter.

2022.8.23(TUE) ~ 24(FRI)

@Barbican Cinema 1, London and more

Main Program

  • Speeches

    Listen to brief speeches from various human rights filmmakers from across the globe.

  • Short-Film

    Watch two short films on various human rights issues in the world.

  • Forum

    A discussion among audience members regarding the films' themes.

  • Feature-Film

    Watch a feature film on a current global human rights issue.

  • Filmmakers' Panel

    Panel discussion with audience led by the feature film's filmmaker.

Featured Filmmakers

Sam Morley

Human rights activist and filmmaker from Australia

Sam uses his talent as a filmmaker to advocate for a better quality of life within indigenous communities in Australia.

Khensani Zulu

Human rights activist from South Africa

Khensani is an award-winning filmmaker who has made over twenty films to enlighten society on gender and sexual based violence in Africa.

Miguel Rodriguez

Human Rights Activist from Mexico

Miguel has made several incredible films that address injustice in Mexico. He campaigns for men, women and children's rights equally.

Elizabeth Brown

Children's rights advocate from USA

Elizabeth's passion for the safety and wellbeing of children emanates through her films. Her works covers wide-ranging issues that impact the lives of children.

Shaban Mohammed

Equality rights activist from Britain

As a muslim refugee living in Britain, Shaban makes films whose themes focus on educating society on religious and racial discrimination, particularly against refugees.

Alexa Martin

Reproductive rights activist from Turkey

Alexa creates documentaries of real women and girls worldwide that have been denied their reproductive rights and the consequences to their lives.
